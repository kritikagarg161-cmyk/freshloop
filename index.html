The Problem

Urban food waste is a $1.2 trillion crisis hiding in plain sight

In dense cities, 40% of food is wasted — trapped behind apartment doors, invisible to neighbors who need it, and dumped into landfills that cities pay billions to operate.

$1,500
Per household / year

The average urban household throws away this much in edible groceries annually — enough to feed a family of four for two months.

8–10%
Of global emissions

Food waste in landfills produces methane — a greenhouse gas 80× more potent than CO₂. Cities are legally mandated to cut this.

$0
Visibility for cities

Municipal governments have zero real-time data on residential food waste. Compliance reporting is manual, inaccurate, and months delayed.

The Solution

A neighborhood "pantry mesh" that rewards you for not wasting food

FreshLoop installs smart redistribution hubs in apartment lobbies and connects them via a hyper-local app. Residents share surplus food, earn rewards, and cities get real-time compliance data.

🧊

Aero-Seal Smart Lockers

Chilled, antimicrobial lockers in apartment lobbies. AI scans use-by dates and freshness on deposit. No smell, no pests, no liability.

📲

Flash-Listing & Matching

When spinach hits the locker, your neighbor on floor 12 gets a "Pesto Night" recipe alert. Hyper-local redistribution within a 3-minute walk.

💳

Eco-Credits Economy

Every gram diverted earns credits redeemable for subway fares, local produce, or building utility offsets. A tiered system prevents hoarding.

📊

Municipal Compliance Layer

Auto-generated, sensor-verified diversion reports for SB 1383, NYC composting mandates, and EU waste framework targets. Plug-and-play for city systems.
